## CHUNK 1 — Billing Cycle

### What is the billing cycle for Axis Bank credit cards?

- **Billing Cycle:** Typically a **30-day period** during which all transactions are accumulated
- **Statement Generation:** At the end of each billing cycle, a monthly statement is generated
- **Statement Date (also called Closing Date):** The date on which the billing cycle ends and the statement is generated
- **Payment Due Date:** Usually **15–18 days after the statement date**, giving you a repayment window

> As per RBI guidelines, Axis Bank must provide cardholders with **at least 15 days (one fortnight)** to make payment after the statement is generated. Axis Bank typically provides more than this minimum.

**Example:**
- Statement date: 5th of each month
- Payment due date: 20th–25th of each month
- Transactions from 6th of previous month to 5th of current month appear on the statement

## CHUNK 2 — Reading Your Credit Card Statement

### What information is on my Axis Bank credit card statement?

Your monthly credit card statement includes:

1. **Statement Period:** Start and end dates of the billing cycle
2. **Statement Date / Closing Date**
3. **Payment Due Date**
4. **Total Outstanding Balance:** Full amount owed including purchases, interest, and fees
5. **Minimum Amount Due (MAD):** Minimum payment required to avoid late fee
6. **Transaction Details:** Itemised list of all debits (purchases, fees, cash advances) and credits (payments, cashback)
7. **Reward Points Summary:** Points earned, redeemed, and balance
8. **Credit Limit & Available Credit**
9. **Interest & Charges Breakdown:** Finance charges, late fees, other charges

Statements are sent via email to your registered email address. You can also view them on the Axis Mobile App or Internet Banking.

## CHUNK 3 — How to Pay Your Credit Card Bill

### How can I pay my Axis Bank Credit Card bill?

**Online payment methods:**
1. **Axis Mobile App:** Cards → Credit Card → Pay Now → Select amount (full/MAD/custom)
2. **Axis Internet Banking:** Payments → Credit Card Payment
3. **NEFT/RTGS:** Use your 16-digit Axis Bank Credit Card number as the account number and Axis Bank IFSC code
4. **IMPS:** Available through any bank's mobile app for instant transfers
5. **UPI:** Pay via Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm etc. using your credit card payment VPA
6. **BillDesk / Online Portals:** Pay from any bank via the payment gateway on axisbank.com

**Offline payment methods:**
1. **Axis Bank Branch:** Cash or cheque at any branch
2. **Axis Bank ATM:** Insert card → Select "Bill Payment" option
3. **Auto-Debit:** Set up standing instructions to auto-debit from your Axis Bank savings account

> NEFT and cheque payments may take 1–2 days to reflect. Pay at least **3 days before the due date** to avoid late fees.

## CHUNK 4 — Auto-Debit Facility

### How do I set up auto-debit for my Axis Bank Credit Card?

Auto-debit automatically deducts your credit card bill from a linked Axis Bank savings/current account on the due date.

**How to set up:**
1. Log in to Axis Mobile App or Internet Banking
2. Go to Credit Card → Settings → Auto Pay
3. Choose auto-debit option:
   - **Full Outstanding Balance** (recommended — avoids all interest)
   - **Minimum Amount Due**
   - **Fixed Custom Amount**
4. Link your Axis Bank account and confirm

**Benefits:**
- Ensures payment is never missed
- Prevents late payment fees
- Protects your CIBIL score

> Auto-debit from a **non-Axis bank account** can be set up via NACH mandate — request the form at a branch or via customer care.

## CHUNK 5 — EMI Conversion

### Can I convert my credit card purchases to EMI?

Yes. Axis Bank offers EMI conversion for eligible transactions.

**Eligibility:**
- Transaction amount must be at least **₹2,500**
- Transaction must be a retail purchase (not cash advance, fuel, gold, or jewellery)
- Card must not be delinquent (overdue)

**EMI Tenure Options:** 3, 6, 9, 12, 18, or 24 months

**Interest Rate:** Approximately **1.5% per month** on the reducing balance (varies by offer and tenure)

**How to Convert:**
1. Axis Mobile App → Cards → EMI → Select Transaction → Choose Tenure
2. SMS/Customer Care conversion for eligible transactions
3. At the point of purchase (for partner merchants offering 0% EMI)

**What happens to credit limit:**
- The EMI principal amount reduces your available credit limit
- As you pay each EMI, your credit limit is partially restored

**Exclusions:** Gold, jewellery, cash withdrawals, balance transfers, and corporate cards are not eligible.

## CHUNK 6 — Zero-Cost / No-Cost EMI

### What is no-cost EMI on Axis Bank credit cards?

**No-cost EMI (0% EMI)** is available at select partner merchants where the interest cost is absorbed by the merchant or Axis Bank as a promotional offer.

- **How it works:** You pay only the principal in monthly instalments — no interest
- **Availability:** Online platforms (Flipkart, Amazon, Myntra), offline retail stores
- **Processing fee:** A processing fee may apply (₹199–₹499 + GST) — check the specific offer
- **Eligibility:** Depends on the merchant, purchase amount, and card type

> During no-cost EMI periods, GST on interest may still be charged in some cases — verify before opting in.

## CHUNK 7 — Balance Transfer

### Can I transfer my balance from another credit card to my Axis Bank card?

Yes. Axis Bank offers a **Balance Transfer (BT)** facility to transfer outstanding balances from other banks' credit cards to your Axis Bank credit card at a lower interest rate.

- **Purpose:** Consolidate high-interest debt from other cards at a lower promotional rate
- **Typical BT Rate:** Lower than standard finance charges — promotional rate varies by offer
- **Tenure:** Usually 3–12 months
- **Minimum Transfer Amount:** ₹5,000 typically
- **Processing Fee:** 1–2% of the transferred amount, or as per specific offer terms

**How to apply:**
- Contact Axis Bank customer care at 1860-419-5555
- Log in to Internet Banking → Credit Card → Balance Transfer → Apply

> Interest charges apply from the transfer date. Evaluate the total cost (processing fee + interest) before transferring.

## CHUNK 8 — What Happens If I Miss a Payment

### What happens if I miss my Axis Bank credit card payment due date?

If you miss the payment due date:

1. **Late Payment Fee:** Charged as per the fee slab (see kb_03 for details)
2. **Interest on Outstanding:** Finance charges apply from the statement date on the full outstanding balance
3. **Grace Period Lost:** You lose the interest-free grace period — interest applies on all new purchases from the transaction date
4. **CIBIL Score Impact:** Late payment is reported to credit bureaus (CIBIL, Experian, CRIF) — negatively affects your credit score
5. **Account Suspension Risk:** Repeated non-payment can lead to card suspension or account being marked delinquent

**If you can't pay in full:**
- At minimum, pay the **Minimum Amount Due (MAD)** to avoid late fees
- Contact Axis Bank customer care to discuss hardship payment plans if you are unable to make payments

## CHUNK 9 — Disputes on Billing Statements

### I see an incorrect charge on my statement — what should I do?

If you notice an incorrect or unrecognised charge on your Axis Bank credit card statement:

1. **Do not wait** — disputes must be filed within **30 days of the statement date**
2. **Raise a dispute through:**
   - Axis Mobile App → Cards → Dispute a Transaction
   - Email: [email protected]
   - Customer Care: 1860-419-5555
   - Submit the **Cardholder Dispute Form** (downloadable from axisbank.com)
3. **Provide details:** Transaction date, merchant name, amount, and reason for dispute
4. **Bank's TAT:** Axis Bank will investigate and resolve within **30 days** of the dispute date
5. **Temporary credit:** A temporary credit may be applied to your account while the investigation is ongoing

> As per RBI guidelines, if your query is not resolved within 30 days, you can escalate to the RBI Ombudsman.
