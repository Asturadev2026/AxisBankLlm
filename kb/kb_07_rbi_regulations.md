# RBI Credit Card Regulations — Customer Rights & Compliance

> **Bot instruction:** Use this file to answer questions about customer rights under RBI rules, what banks are required to do by law, how interest is calculated as per RBI norms, and escalation rights. This applies to all banks in India including Axis Bank.

---

## CHUNK 1 — Overview of RBI Master Direction 2022

### What is the RBI Master Direction on Credit Cards?

The **Reserve Bank of India (RBI) Master Direction — Credit Card and Debit Card – Issuance and Conduct Directions, 2022** was issued on **April 21, 2022** and became effective from **July 1, 2022**.

This direction consolidates all earlier circulars and guidelines on credit cards and debit cards into a single comprehensive regulation applicable to all scheduled commercial banks and NBFCs issuing credit cards in India.

**Key objectives:**
- Protect customers from unfair practices
- Ensure transparency in interest and fee disclosure
- Regulate the issuance, activation, and closure of cards
- Establish grievance redressal standards

## CHUNK 2 — Unsolicited Cards and Prior Consent

### Can a bank issue me a credit card without my consent?

**No.** As per RBI Master Direction 2022:

- Banks are **prohibited from issuing unsolicited (unrequested) credit cards**
- **Prior and explicit consent** from the customer is mandatory before issuing a card
- If an unsolicited card is received and the customer has NOT consented to activate it:
  - The bank must **close the account within 7 working days** of the customer's request
  - **No charges** can be levied on the customer for a card they did not request

**Your right:** If you receive a credit card you did not apply for, you have the right to request closure at no cost within 30 days of receipt.

## CHUNK 3 — Key Fact Statement (KFS)

### What is a Key Fact Statement and am I entitled to receive one?

Yes. Under RBI rules, every credit card issuer **must provide a Key Fact Statement (KFS)** to every cardholder.

**What the KFS must contain:**
- Annual Percentage Rate (APR) for purchases, cash advances, balance transfers
- All applicable fees and charges
- Interest-free period details
- Minimum payment warning and consequences of paying only the minimum
- Grievance redressal contact information

**When you receive it:** Along with your credit card welcome kit (physical or digital).

**Your right:** You can request a copy of the KFS at any time from Axis Bank customer care or download it from the bank's website.

## CHUNK 4 — Annualised Percentage Rate (APR)

### What is APR and how is it different from the monthly interest rate?

**APR (Annualised Percentage Rate)** is the total cost of credit expressed as a yearly rate, including all fees and charges, not just the stated monthly interest rate.

**Why APR matters:**
- Monthly rate of 3.6% looks small but = **43.2% APR annually**
- APR includes processing fees, annual fees (amortised), and other charges, making it a true cost comparison tool

**RBI requirement:** Banks must quote APR separately for:
- Retail purchases
- Cash advances
- Balance transfers
- Late payment scenarios

**Where to find it:** On your Key Fact Statement, on the bank's website under each card's fee schedule, and on your monthly credit card statement.

## CHUNK 5 — Billing Statement and Minimum Payment Warning

### What are banks required to include in credit card billing statements under RBI rules?

Under RBI Master Direction 2022, credit card statements must include:

1. **Minimum Amount Due (MAD)** prominently displayed
2. **Consequences of paying only MAD** — a clear warning that paying only the minimum amount will result in higher interest charges and longer debt repayment period
3. **Total outstanding balance and unbilled transactions**
4. **Interest-free period remaining** (if applicable)
5. **Payment due date** — at least **15 days (one fortnight)** from the statement date
6. **Full APR and charges applicable**

> Banks must send statements via email or mail. If a customer raises a query about any statement item, the bank must resolve and provide documentary evidence within **30 days**.

## CHUNK 6 — Credit Limit and Over-Limit Rules

### Can the bank increase my credit limit without my consent?

**No.** As per RBI regulations:

- Banks **cannot enhance credit limits without the cardholder's explicit consent**
- Customers have the right to **opt out of unsolicited credit limit increases**
- The bank must have a formal process for customers to set their own credit limit (including requesting a reduction)

**Your right:** If your credit limit was increased without your consent, you can request reversal and ask the bank to maintain the original limit.

**Over-limit facility:**
- Must also be based on prior consent
- Over-limit charges can only be levied if the customer has opted in for the over-limit facility

## CHUNK 7 — Card Closure Rights

### Can I close my Axis Bank credit card at any time? What are my rights?

**Yes.** Under RBI rules:

- Cardholders have the **unconditional right to close their credit card account at any time**
- Banks **cannot insist on a minimum usage period** before closure
- Closure must be processed within **7 working days** of the request
- **No closure fee** can be charged unless the cardholder has unpaid dues

**What happens at closure:**
1. Clear all outstanding dues (balance must be zero)
2. Redeem pending reward points (points are forfeited after closure)
3. Cancel all auto-debits/ECS linked to the card
4. Get written confirmation of closure from the bank

> If the bank delays closure beyond 7 working days, they are in violation of RBI guidelines — escalate to the RBI Ombudsman.

## CHUNK 8 — Grievance Redressal Under RBI Rules

### What is my right to complain under RBI regulations?

**Bank's obligation:**
- Every bank must have a **Board-approved grievance redressal policy** for credit cards
- Banks must **publicize** their grievance mechanism on their website, monthly statements, and through other communications
- Complaints must be acknowledged and resolved within a reasonable timeframe

**Escalation timeline:**
1. **Internal — Bank Customer Care:** Raise complaint via call/email/app
2. **Internal — Nodal Officer:** If unresolved within 30 days
3. **RBI Ombudsman:**
   - File online at: **cms.rbi.org.in**
   - If the bank has not responded within **30 days**, or if the response is unsatisfactory
   - The Ombudsman can direct the bank to compensate the customer

**RBI Ombudsman can award:**
- Reversal of unauthorized charges
- Compensation for mental agony and harassment
- Awards up to **₹20 lakh** (₹1 crore in extreme cases involving fraud)

## CHUNK 9 — Interest on Unauthorized Transactions and Delays

### What does RBI say about interest on disputed/unauthorized transactions?

Under RBI guidelines:

- **If a transaction is disputed,** the bank should not continue charging interest on the disputed amount during the investigation period — they may apply a provisional credit
- **If the bank wrongly levies charges:** The bank must reverse the charges with interest from the date of wrong debit
- **Reporting timeline for zero liability:** Customer must report unauthorized transactions promptly:
  - Within 3 working days: **Zero liability** (full reversal)
  - 4–7 working days: **Limited liability** (capped at ₹5,000–₹25,000 depending on account type)
  - Beyond 7 working days: Subject to bank policy

> This policy applies only when the fraud was NOT due to the customer's own negligence (e.g., sharing OTP or PIN).

## CHUNK 10 — Telemarketing and Harassment Rules

### What does RBI say about unsolicited calls from credit card companies?

Under RBI Master Direction 2022 and TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority of India) regulations:

- **Telemarketing calls** can only be made between **10:00 AM and 7:00 PM** — no calls outside this window
- Banks and their agents **cannot use coercive methods** to recover dues
- Calling customers at odd hours or harassing them for payments is a violation of RBI's Fair Practice Code
- Customers have the right to register on the **Do Not Disturb (DND) registry** to block promotional calls

**How to register for DND:**
- Call 1909 or SMS "START 0" to 1909 from your registered mobile number
