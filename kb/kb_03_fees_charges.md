The joining fee is the first-year annual fee. Subsequent years incur the regular annual fee.

## CHUNK 2 — Annual Fee Waiver

### How can I get the annual fee waived on my Axis Bank Credit Card?

Many Axis Bank cards offer fee waivers based on annual spends:

- **Magnus:** Annual fee waived on spends ≥ ₹25 lakh per year
- **Atlas:** No fee waiver, but the milestone reward of 2,500 EDGE Miles (worth ₹5,000) effectively offsets the fee for eligible spenders
- **ACE / Flipkart / Rewards:** Fee waiver thresholds may apply — check your specific card's T&C
- **SELECT:** Fee waiver based on spend milestones defined in the welcome kit

**How to claim waiver:** The waiver is applied automatically at the time of annual fee billing if the spend criterion is met in the preceding year.

## CHUNK 3 — Interest Rate (Finance Charges)

### What interest rate does Axis Bank charge on credit cards?

Axis Bank charges a monthly interest rate (finance charge) ranging from **1.5% to 3.6% per month**, depending on the card variant.

This translates to an **Annualised Percentage Rate (APR) of 18% to 43.2% per year**.

**When interest is charged:**
1. When you do not pay the **full outstanding amount** by the payment due date
2. When you make a **cash withdrawal** from an ATM using your credit card (interest accrues from the date of transaction — no grace period)
3. When you have outstanding dues from previous months (interest is calculated on the full statement balance, not just the unpaid portion)
4. If you opt for a **balance transfer** — applicable rate may differ

> As per RBI guidelines, Axis Bank must publish APR clearly for each transaction type on its website and in the Key Fact Statement.

## CHUNK 4 — Interest-Free Grace Period

### Is there an interest-free period on Axis Bank credit cards?

Yes. Axis Bank credit cards offer an **interest-free period of up to 50 days** on retail purchases, provided:

- You **pay the full outstanding balance** (not just the minimum due) by the payment due date, AND
- You have **no cash advance outstanding** on the card

If you have any outstanding balance or cash advance, the grace period does NOT apply to new transactions — interest accrues from the date of each new purchase.

## CHUNK 5 — Late Payment Fee

### What is the late payment fee for Axis Bank credit cards?

Late payment fees are charged when the **minimum amount due (MAD)** is not paid by the due date.

| Outstanding Balance | Late Payment Fee |
|---|---|
| Less than ₹500 | Nil |
| ₹500 – ₹5,000 | ₹500 |
| ₹5,001 – ₹10,000 | ₹750 |
| ₹10,001 – ₹25,000 | ₹900 |
| ₹25,001 – ₹50,000 | ₹1,100 |
| Above ₹50,000 | ₹1,300 |

**Exceptions:**
- Late payment fee is **waived** for Burgundy Private and PRIMUS cards
- Olympus card: ₹100 if balance is above ₹2,000

> GST is applicable on late payment fees. Repeated late payments also impact your CIBIL score negatively.

## CHUNK 6 — Cash Advance Fee

### What fees apply when I withdraw cash using my Axis Bank Credit Card?

Cash advances (ATM withdrawals using a credit card) attract:

- **Cash Advance Fee:** 2.5% of the amount withdrawn, subject to a minimum of ₹500
- **Interest:** Finance charges apply **from the date of transaction** — there is no grace period for cash advances
- **ATM usage:** Available at any Visa/Mastercard ATM in India and abroad

> Cash advances are expensive due to immediate interest accrual. It is advisable to use this facility only in emergencies.

## CHUNK 7 — Foreign Currency Transaction Fee

### What charges apply for international/foreign currency transactions?

When you use your Axis Bank credit card for transactions in a foreign currency:

- **Forex Markup Fee:** 3.5% of the transaction amount for most cards
- **Exceptions:**
  - Burgundy Private card: Nil forex markup
  - PRIMUS card: Nil forex markup
  - Magnus: Reduced forex markup — check card T&C

> Foreign currency transactions are converted to INR using the prevailing exchange rate on the transaction date. The forex markup is charged on the converted INR amount.

## CHUNK 9 — Minimum Amount Due

### How is the Minimum Amount Due (MAD) calculated?

The **Minimum Amount Due (MAD)** is the smallest payment you must make by the due date to keep your account in good standing.

**Typical MAD calculation:**
- 5% of the total outstanding balance, OR
- Total of all EMI installments + interest + fees + any amount overdue from previous cycles
- Whichever is higher

> **Important:** Paying only the MAD does NOT avoid interest charges. Interest will be levied on the remaining outstanding amount. To avoid all interest, always pay the **full outstanding balance** by the due date.
