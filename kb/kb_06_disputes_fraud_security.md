## CHUNK 1 — What to Do Immediately After Fraud

### I see a fraudulent transaction on my Axis Bank Credit Card — what should I do immediately?

**Immediate steps (act within minutes/hours):**

1. **Block your card immediately:**
   - Axis Mobile App → Cards → Block Card (instant)
   - SMS: Send "BLOCK XXXX" (last 4 digits of card) to 5676782
   - Call 1860-419-5555 (24x7 helpline)

2. **Do NOT share your OTP, CVV, card PIN, or internet banking password** with anyone — Axis Bank will never ask for these

3. **Document the transaction:** Note the date, time, merchant name, and amount of the suspicious transaction

4. **File a dispute** within 30 days of the statement date (see CHUNK 3 below)

5. **File a police complaint** for significant fraudulent transactions — keep the FIR copy for your records

> The faster you report, the better your chances of a full reversal. As per RBI guidelines, your liability may be zero for fraud reported promptly.

## CHUNK 2 — Types of Credit Card Fraud

### What are the common types of credit card fraud I should know about?

**Common fraud types:**

- **Unauthorized card-present transaction:** Someone physically uses your stolen or cloned card
- **Card-not-present (CNP) fraud:** Your card details (number, CVV, expiry) are used for online transactions without the physical card
- **Phishing:** Fraudulent emails/SMSs/calls impersonating Axis Bank to steal your credentials
- **Vishing:** Fraudulent phone calls claiming to be bank representatives asking for OTP or card details
- **Skimming:** Physical device attached to ATMs or POS terminals that copies your card data
- **SIM swap fraud:** Fraudsters convince your mobile operator to issue a new SIM with your number, gaining access to OTPs

**Red flags:**
- OTP received for a transaction you didn't initiate
- Unexpected decline of your card at a merchant
- Statement showing transactions in a city you haven't visited

## CHUNK 3 — Filing a Transaction Dispute (Chargeback)

### How do I file a dispute for an incorrect or fraudulent transaction?

**Eligibility:** Disputes must be filed within **30 days of the statement date**.

**Common dispute reasons:**
- Transaction not initiated by cardholder (unauthorized)
- Goods/services ordered but not received
- Duplicate billing (charged twice for same transaction)
- Wrong amount charged
- Cancelled subscription/membership still being billed
- Merchant not processing a refund

**How to file a dispute:**
1. **Axis Mobile App:** Cards → Transaction History → Select Transaction → Dispute
2. **Email:** Send details + Cardholder Dispute Form to [email protected]
3. **Customer Care:** Call 1860-419-5555 and raise a dispute request
4. **Online Portal:** Visit application.axis.bank.in → Report Fraud/Dispute

**What to include in your dispute:**
- Your name, card number (last 4 digits), registered mobile number
- Transaction date, amount, and merchant name
- Reason for dispute with supporting documentation (e.g., cancellation confirmation, receipt showing different amount)

## CHUNK 4 — Chargeback Process and Timeline

### How does the Axis Bank chargeback investigation work?

**Step-by-step process:**

1. **Dispute Filed:** You raise a dispute within 30 days of statement date
2. **Acknowledgement:** Axis Bank acknowledges your dispute (typically within 1–2 business days)
3. **Temporary Credit:** A provisional credit may be applied to your account while investigation is ongoing (amount is blocked, not spendable)
4. **Investigation:** Axis Bank contacts the merchant's bank (acquirer) to verify the transaction — typically takes 15–45 days
5. **Merchant Response:** If the merchant cannot prove the transaction was legitimate, the chargeback is resolved in your favour
6. **Final Resolution:**
   - **Chargeback approved:** Temporary credit becomes permanent; amount fully reversed
   - **Chargeback rejected:** Temporary credit is reversed; your account is debited

**Timeline:** RBI requires resolution within **30 working days** of dispute filing.

> If unresolved within 30 days, you can escalate to the **RBI Ombudsman**.

## CHUNK 6 — Zero Liability Policy (RBI Guidelines)

### Am I liable for unauthorized transactions on my Axis Bank Credit Card?

As per **RBI's guidelines on unauthorized electronic transactions**, your liability depends on where the fault lies:

| Scenario | Your Liability |
|---|---|
| Fraud due to bank's negligence/system breach | **ZERO** — full reversal |
| Third-party fraud (no contribution from customer or bank) — reported within 3 working days | **ZERO** — full reversal |
| Third-party fraud — reported within 4–7 working days | Limited liability (capped amount per card type) |
| Third-party fraud — reported after 7 working days | Liability as per bank's approved policy |
| Customer's own negligence (shared PIN/OTP) | **Customer is liable** |

> Key principle: **Report fraud as quickly as possible.** The sooner you report, the better your protection. Sharing OTP or PIN with anyone — including someone claiming to be from Axis Bank — makes you liable.

## CHUNK 7 — Card Security Best Practices

### How can I keep my Axis Bank Credit Card secure?

**Recommended security practices:**

**Online Security:**
- Never share your Card Number, CVV, Expiry Date, OTP, or PIN with anyone — Axis Bank will never ask for these
- Always check that the website URL starts with "https://" before entering card details
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for online transactions
- Enable transaction alerts (SMS + email) for every transaction

**Physical Card Security:**
- Keep your card in a safe place; do not leave it unattended
- Shield the keypad when entering your PIN at ATMs or POS terminals
- Check ATM machines for suspicious attachments (skimming devices) before inserting your card
- Memorize your PIN — do not write it on the card or keep it with the card

**Digital Controls (via Axis Mobile App):**
- Enable/disable **international transactions** (turn on only when travelling)
- Enable/disable **online transactions**
- Enable/disable **contactless payments**
- Set **transaction limits** for specific channels

## CHUNK 8 — Phishing and Social Engineering

### How do I identify and report phishing attempts related to Axis Bank?

**Identifying phishing:**
- Emails/SMSs with urgent messages like "Your card will be blocked — verify now"
- Links that look like axisbank.com but are slightly different (e.g., axisbank-verify.com)
- Calls claiming to be from Axis Bank asking for OTP, CVV, or card number
- Offers that seem too good to be true (reward redemptions requiring card details)

**Axis Bank will NEVER ask you for:**
- Full card number
- CVV
- ATM PIN
- Internet banking password
- OTP

**How to report phishing:**
- Email: [email protected]
- Call: 1860-419-5555
- Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in (India national portal)

## CHUNK 9 — Grievance Escalation for Fraud/Disputes

### What if Axis Bank doesn't resolve my fraud complaint satisfactorily?

**Escalation path:**

1. **Level 1 — Axis Bank Customer Care:** Call 1860-419-5555 or email [email protected]
2. **Level 2 — Axis Bank Nodal Officer:** Email your complaint to Axis Bank's designated Nodal Officer (details available on axisbank.com under Grievance Redressal)
3. **Level 3 — RBI Banking Ombudsman:**
   - If your complaint is not resolved within **30 days** by Axis Bank
   - File online at: cms.rbi.org.in
   - The Ombudsman can award compensation up to ₹20 lakh
4. **Consumer Forum:** Under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 for unresolved disputes

> Keep all written communication, complaint reference numbers, and transaction records when escalating.
